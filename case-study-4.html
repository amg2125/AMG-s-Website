School of Comm VR Tour

  • Media Type: Virtual Reality
  • Group Project
  • Tools Used: Unity, Illustrator, Audacity

This virtual reality experience was created for a course called "Immersive Storytelling". For the project, students had to create a VR experience that both told a story and was interactive. The students that I worked with on this project were Daniele Montefiori, Jack Ketchum, and Lejie Lin.

Development

Choosing the Concept

I chose to adapt a concept I had developed for a previous course: "Death of Innocence", a modern retelling of "All Quiet on the Western Front" set during the Russo-Ukariainian War. The story follows Boris Sperkaz, a Ukrainian college student who is drafted into the army at the outbreak of the war. I already had character designs and a basic outline, making this an ideal starting point.

Script and Storyboard

I structured the video as a trailer to fit the project's time limit. I wrote a script in which Boris narrates how his life changed when the war came, and created a storyboard showing scenes from before and during the war. I revised the script and storyboard as the project progressed, adjusting for pacing, visuals, and whatever was realistic to animate in the time I had.

The first page of the storyboard for "Death of Innocence"

Asset Creation

I created all the assets for the film—characters, props, and backgrounds—in Adobe Illustrator. For Boris, I referenced his previous character design sketches and created multiple models for different scenes and expressions. Since some models took longer to create than others, I started animating fully completed scenes while continuing to build the remaining assets in others to stay on schedule.

Boris' character model open in Illustrator

A background showing the skyline of Kyiv for a scene in "Death of Innocence"

Narration and Sound Design

While creating the visuals, I also collected multiple sound effects from YouTube and stock sound libraries to fit the various scenes. For the narration, I used the AI text-to-speech generator ElevenLabs to voice Boris, since I lacked the time and resources to find a voice actor or record it myself. I copied the script into Elevenlabs and generated the audio clips with a fitting voice, which saved time and kept the film's tone consistent.

The film's narration was generated using a website called "ElevenLabs"

Animating in After Effects

Animation was the most crucial and time-intensive part of the project. I carefully parented and layered all the assets, applied keyframes for movements, and utilized class-taught techniques throughout the process. One complex scene involved multiple Ukrainian soldiers running into battle, and to speed up the animation process I used a plugin called Duik Angela to rig the soldiers. This was my first time using Duik and I ran into problems with the rigging, but with trial and error, and help from the professor, I made it work.

Editing and Final Delivery

After animating, I assembled the short in Adobe Premier Pro. The editing process was straightforward since I only needed to arrange and cut the scenes together. After that, I exported the final film and screened it on the final day of class.

Final Deliverable

Takeaways

This project allowed me to learn more about After Effects and its various plugins. Duik Angela was challenging to use, but learning to use it gave me insight into rigging workflows and shortcuts in After Effects. I also learned that AI tools like Elevenlabs can be useful timesavers, especially for solo projects. Lastly, animating while assets were still in progress got me into an efficient workflow, allowing me to manage my time better and helping me meet the deadline.
