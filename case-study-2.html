Atzok the Deserter

  • Media Type: Presentation
  • Individual Project
  • Tools Used: Adobe Photoshop, MediBang Paint

This project was created for a class in 2D Character Design, and it was the final project of said class. For the project, students had to come up an original story concept and create three characters (a protagonist, a secondary character, and an antagonist) for that concept. Students had to design these characters, revise their designs based on feedback from the professor and classmates, and implement content learned in class into the development process. The project was to be turned in as a presentation that gave an overview of the characters, the story, and their development.

Development

The first step in this project's development was coming up with a story concept. I wanted my story to be based in science fiction because that was one of my favorite genres, but I couldn't come up with a story concept at first. So I looked for inspiration both from the internet and from some of my favorite sci-fi stories, most notably Star Wars. After taking in some inspiration, I got the idea for a story about an alien soldier who deserts his post, flees to an isolated planet, and years later finds himself being hunted by men from the same army he deserted. I took this idea and fleshed it out further, creating a grounded story that was set against the backdrop of a much larger universe, in the same vein as "A New Hope."

After settling on a story concept, I began working on the characters, starting with the protagonist. The main character of this story was Atzok, an alien belonging to a highly militaristic race of reptilians called the "Klorn." I had never drawn reptilian characters before, so I struggled initally to come up with Atzok's design. However, I went to the professor for some guidance and looked up references of reptiles in the real world and from fiction to help me, and I able to come up with a rough design for Atzok. I then refined his design based on feedback in class and came up with multiple sketches of Atzok in different poses and expressions.

After settling on Atzok's design, I moved on to creating the secondary character. I wanted Atzok to have a robot named D4A5 as his companion, but just like with Atzok, I struggled to come up with a design for the robot. I wanted the robot's design to have a vintage feel to it, so I looked at images of robots from old sci-fi magazines from the 1970s and 1980s as well as concept art from the original Star Wars trilogy for inspiration. Yet even with this inspiration and more assistance from the professor, D4A5's design was a bit more difficult to nail down than Atzok's. I created multiple concept sketchess for D4's design before I finally settled on one that I liked.

The final character I had to design was the antagonist. The antagonist I created was called Trosk, who was an old comrade who fought with Atzok in the Klorn Army. Trosk was like a foil to Atzok because he never deserted the Klorn Army and remained loyal to it long after the war. Initially, Trosk's design wasn't that much different from Atzok's, but the professor suggested that I make Trosk look more menacing and battle-hardened to contrast with Atzok, and I took this advice and came up with a much better design. After finalizing Trosk's design, I put all the sketches of all three characters together into a Photoshop file and created the final presentation. The slideshow was incredibly detailed, as it explained each of the characters, showed the evolution of their designs, and showed them in different poses and situations.

Takeaways

I learned a lot about video editing from working on this project. One of the biggest lessons I learned was that video editing requires a lot of cutting, shortening, and deleting of footage. I had to make many changes to the footage that I didn't originally plan on, and this was mainly due to having to compress everything to make sure it fit the length of the song. Another big takeaway I took from this was that sometimes projects need another round of work before they look satisfactory. My original cut of the music video was good, but there were ways it could be improved. By revisiting the project and making a few more changes, like adding zooming transitions and shortening more clips, I was able to create a better version of the video.
